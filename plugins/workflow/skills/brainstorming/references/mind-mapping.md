# Mind Mapping

## What It Is
A visual thinking technique that radiates ideas outward from a central topic, creating a branching tree of associations. Each branch spawns sub-branches, revealing connections and structure in free-form thinking.

## When to Use
- Exploring a complex topic with many dimensions
- Need to see relationships between ideas
- Blank slate situation where the problem space is large and undefined
- Planning the structure of a project, document, or system

## When NOT to Use
- A simple, focused question that doesn't need decomposition
- Already have a clear list of ideas that need evaluation
- Sequential process design (flowcharts work better)

## Process Steps
1. **Place the central topic** — Write the core theme or question in the center.
2. **Identify main branches** — What are the 3-6 major dimensions or categories? These become first-level branches.
3. **Expand each branch** — For each main branch, free-associate sub-topics. Don't overthink — write whatever comes to mind.
4. **Go deeper** — Pick the most interesting sub-branches and expand them further (third level).
5. **Find connections** — Look for cross-links between branches. Ideas that connect across categories are often the most valuable.
6. **Identify gaps** — Which branches are thin? These represent unexplored areas worth investigating.

## Facilitator Prompts
- "What are the major dimensions of this topic?"
- "Let's explore the [branch] direction further — what sub-topics come to mind?"
- "I notice [branch A] and [branch B] seem related. How do they connect?"
- "This branch is thin — let's spend a moment brainstorming more sub-ideas here."
- "Which branch excites you the most? Let's go deeper there."
- "Are there any dimensions we're missing entirely?"
